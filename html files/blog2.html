Creators and Communities: Why Their Survival depends on Co-Existence

Content Creators today are churning heavy on a lot of aspects being a small business themselves. One of them is building a strong community. If we look into things closely, the basic economics which we observe around us never changes. For Example, when we want to buy groceries, priority is to buy the necessities. Once we reach the rack, our eyes straight away look for the brand we have been using on a regular basis. And, if we can’t see it, we do get a bit uncomfortable. But how did we as consumers start following that single brand in itself? So many things get factored in making that particular decision

In this article we will be looking into those aspects of how they are directly linked to building a community for content creators, and its sheer utilisation

Fan meeting of Put Your Head on My Shoulder series

  1. Relevance

    This is something which attracts the consumer. Are you supplying what is the need of the market, as of now, or have been for a long time?
    The relevance of your presence plays a vital role in tapping the market. We always hear mixed reviews about Walmart. You like it or not, we are always relieved to have one around us. The community needs a safe and cheap grocery marketplace to buy as many things as possible. Walmart is creating awareness about how you will always find one around you. Its relevance is associated with its locality and variety of items it sells

    Same goes for the creators. When you create your content, it is always scaled on two aspects, long term and short term, or as we say, just trend worthy. Your content’s relevance is tested on its longevity. How are you reaching the right people irrespective of what the trend you are on?
    The communication, design, relevant motive around the said genre plays key roles in retention of the user on your profile.


  2. Consistency

    We as a consumer, or as a human in general will always appreciate the things we love to stay the same for a longer course. Our human mind cannot sustain with continuous change. Because, when a viewer is consuming your content, they are always consuming for the peace they crave in that time. Like, when you step out to get yourself a Bagel, ideally you would always prefer to go at the same lorry. This allows your mind to settle down on the exact taste you have been habitual of now.
    The consistency of the quality of content and the same time period of it being posted, slowly allows the consumer to stay connected. We as humans have evolved with television.
    Television Networks always telecast shows at the same time every week and maintain story lines until you are up for some dramatics. Everyone has grown up like this. And, now it is heavily wired in our system. To look for a consistent outlook for peace. Our lives are already so chaotic that consumers will like to stay with you, to feel sane and look out for the day and time of your content release.


  3. Quality

    This is the crucial aspect to build your community. Slowly and steadily building a range of good quality content. This should reflect in your work by the passage of time. The set of content you produce is like your very own portfolio, it defines you. This also means exploring new ways to create content, trying different ways to make your content more relevant and having a consistent push towards growth. Community is built over belief and the word-of-mouth spreads over time. But, this can sustain the amount of time you spend on making something beautiful.

    Pull up your socks, being a content creator is amazing, it’s beautiful, and the love from your own community will always make you smile bright.
